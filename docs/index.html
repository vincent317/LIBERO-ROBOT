Robotics machine learning case study

LIBERO Robot Learning

I trained and debugged LeRobot ACT policies for simulated robot manipulation, starting with one bowl-placement task and scaling to a shared policy across all ten LIBERO-Spatial tasks. This page focuses on the experiment reasoning, not just the final metric.

Result Summary

  • 82% best single-task success: 41 / 50 benchmark episodes.
  • 69.4% best verified multitask mean across ten spatial tasks.
  • 62,250 multitask frames converted into a local LeRobot dataset.
  • 10 LIBERO-Spatial tasks conditioned by one shared ACT policy.

Thinking Process

  1. Make ACT run end to end. I converted LIBERO HDF5 demonstrations into a LeRobot image-backed dataset, then wrote local training and evaluation entrypoints so I could iterate without fighting import, cache, and environment issues.
  2. Inspect failures instead of only tuning hyperparameters. Early rollouts usually reached the objects, but the policy failed around grasp acquisition and release timing. That shifted the next experiment toward the high-value moments in the trajectory.
  3. Change the data sampling around the observed failure. Oversampling gripper-transition windows improved the single-task benchmark from 44% to 62%, which supported the diagnosis that the model needed more signal around contact and handoff moments.
  4. Ablate the objective. Reducing ACT KL weight from 10.0 to 5.0 lifted success to 82%. Lowering it further to 2.0 dropped success to 76%, so the best setting came from an ablation rather than a monotonic assumption.
  5. Scale to multitask and treat negative results as evidence. A task-conditioned policy worked well on several variants, but simple oversampling of weak tasks collapsed the mean from 69.4% to 22.0%. That made the imbalance explanation less convincing and motivated cleaner architecture ablations.

Task 05 Hard Case

Task 05 stayed weak even after a focused rescue attempt. I keep this failure case in the project because it shows the next debugging question: whether the issue is task-specific train/eval mismatch, data conversion, or architecture rather than simple undertraining.

Implementation Work

  • Converted LIBERO demonstrations into local LeRobot datasets with agent-view RGB, wrist RGB, robot state, actions, and task IDs.
  • Built local ACT training scripts with AMP support, checkpointing, validation, and timing logs.
  • Evaluated on LIBERO pruned initial states, saved rollout videos, and tracked task-level success across the benchmark.
